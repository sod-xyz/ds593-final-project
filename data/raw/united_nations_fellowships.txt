United Nations Fellowships (Multiple countries)

Overview:
United Nations Fellowships. United Nations fellowships are training or fellowship activities designed to build capacity for qualified individuals, often from developing countries or member states. Examples include UN/DESA training and fellowship programmes and the United Nations International Law Fellowship Programme, which provides training for qualified professionals such as government officials and teachers of international law from developing countries and emerging economies.

Degree Level:
- Professional training / Fellowship

Language Requirement:
- Requirement type: required
- Details: Typically requires professional working proficiency in English or other UN languages

Funding:
- Funding type: varies
- Details: Funding varies by fellowship; some provide travel, training costs, stipend, or subsistence support.
